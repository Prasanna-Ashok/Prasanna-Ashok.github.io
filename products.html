CHILLI VARIETIES



There are several varieties of Indian Chilli - the most popular are Sanam/334, Teja, Byadgi, Wrinkle/273 and Wonder Hot. Indian chillies have many uses including: food, confectionery, cosmetics, pharmaceuticals, farm protection and paint manufacturing. Guntur Red Dry chillies are available with different levels of spiciness ranging from high to sweet.



The color from deep red to light red. Harvest of the chillies in India is between Jan to April. IPM - Integrated Pest Management is an approach which is ecological to control the usage of the pesticides in chilli farming. Ipm chillies are popular in Europe and Japan for its premium quality and pricing.
